Xymon Code of Conduct
====================

Like the technical community as a whole, the Xymon community is made up of professionals and volunteers from around the world, contributing across development, maintenance, support, and knowledge sharing.

Diversity is a strength, but it can also lead to misunderstandings. This Code of Conduct defines baseline expectations to ensure a respectful, productive, and inclusive environment for everyone involved in the project.

This document is not an exhaustive list of prohibited behavior. It is intended as a practical guide for constructive participation.

Scope
-----

This Code of Conduct applies to all communication spaces, platforms, and activities managed or operated by the Xymon project, whether online or in person.

Standards of Behavior
---------------------

All participants are expected to follow these principles:

- Be friendly and patient.
  Assume good intent and allow room for learning and mistakes.

- Be welcoming.
  Participation is open to anyone who engages constructively and respectfully.

- Be considerate.
  Your actions and decisions affect others and the long-term health of the project. Keep in mind that this is a global community and that communication styles may differ.

- Be respectful.
  Disagreement is normal and can be productive. Disrespect, hostility, or personal attacks are not acceptable.

- Maintain professional conduct.
  Any behavior that undermines personal safety, dignity, or the ability of others to participate constructively is not acceptable.

- Resolve disagreements constructively.
  Focus on facts and shared goals. Seek to understand differing viewpoints and work toward solutions rather than assigning blame.
